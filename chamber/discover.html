This steel framed sign spans over Washinton Boulevard on the River Bridge and has neon lights in the evening. It was built in 1936 by the Ypung Electric Sign Company under the direction of Mayor Harmon W. Peery.

The Ogden Temple was announced in 1967, but was officialy dedicated on January 18th 1972 but Jpseph Fielding Smith. The original design was very similar to the Provo temple, but was then renevated in 2010 as seen in photo.

This statue is of a Mountain Man and Traooer named Peter Skene Ogden who the city is named after. One of the first known settlers to step foot in Ogden

The Municipal Building was built between 1938 to 1940. The top floor used to to be a jail, but was later moved to a building next to the Municipal building. This building was also listed on the National Register of Historic places.

Malan's Waterfall is located on the east side of Ogden on Malans Peak. The hiking trail to this waterfall remains one of Ogdens most popular hikes and offers excelent views of the city.
